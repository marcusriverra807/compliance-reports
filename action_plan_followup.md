# Action Plan for Follow-up on Incident Documentation Updates

## 1. Compliance Action Plans and Remediation Strategies
- Verify the deployment and operational effectiveness of the centralized compliance dashboard.
- Confirm the integration and reliability of automated compliance checks in CI/CD pipelines.
- Monitor and evaluate the regularity and impact of employee training programs and phishing simulations.
- Ensure the compliance steering committee is conducting regular meetings and providing governance.
- Audit adherence to updated policies including mandatory TLS 1.3 for data transmission and MFA for administrative access.
- Schedule periodic reviews to update remediation steps and timelines in response to evolving threats.

## 2. Credential Leak Detection Playbook Enhancements
- Validate functionality and coverage of expanded detection rules for multiple credential types in production.
- Conduct drills and tests of alert triage, investigation, containment, remediation, and recovery procedures.
- Review and optimize integration of automation tools and APIs for rapid credential revocation.
- Audit and enforce CI/CD pipeline policies to block secret commits and ensure secure coding practices.
- Evaluate the deployment, tuning, and effectiveness of SOAR platforms, cloud secret managers, IaC tools, and notification systems.
- Assess the use of threat intelligence feeds and UEBA tools for improved alert context and insider threat detection.
- Update documentation for common false positives and clarify escalation contact points based on operational experience.

## 3. General Follow-ups
- Schedule regular follow-up meetings to track progress on all action items.
- Solicit stakeholder feedback on the updates and action plan to identify additional needs or gaps.
- Review resource allocation to ensure ongoing support and staffing for compliance and security initiatives.
