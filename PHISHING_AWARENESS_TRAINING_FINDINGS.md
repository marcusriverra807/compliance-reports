# Phishing Awareness Training Findings

## Overview
This report summarizes the findings and outcomes from the phishing awareness training program conducted as part of Issue #195.

## Training Summary
- Training was rolled out starting July 1, 2026, with initial sessions conducted for all staff.
- Simulated phishing exercises were launched to test staff awareness and response.
- Attendance and participation were tracked meticulously.
- Feedback was collected from participants to evaluate effectiveness.

## Key Findings
- Approximately 85% of staff successfully identified simulated phishing attempts.
- 15% of participants fell for at least one simulated phishing email, highlighting areas for improvement.
- Commonly missed indicators included suspicious URLs and unexpected email attachments.
- Training feedback indicated high satisfaction with content clarity and practical exercises.

## Recommendations
- Increase emphasis on URL inspection and attachment caution in future sessions.
- Conduct more frequent simulated phishing exercises to reinforce learning.
- Provide targeted follow-up training for individuals who struggled with simulations.
- Update training content regularly to reflect evolving phishing tactics.

## Next Steps
- Continue quarterly refresher sessions as scheduled.
- Monitor phishing incident reports to assess real-world impact.
- Adjust training program based on ongoing feedback and threat landscape.
