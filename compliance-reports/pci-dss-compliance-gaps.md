# PCI-DSS Compliance Gaps Report

## Overview
This report summarizes the compliance gaps identified in relation to the PCI-DSS standards, including remediation timelines and recommendations for addressing each gap.

## Compliance Gaps
1. **Gap 1: Lack of Strong Access Control Measures**  
   - **Description**: User access controls are insufficient, leading to unauthorized access risks.  
   - **Remediation Timeline**: 3 months  
   - **Recommendations**: Implement role-based access controls (RBAC) and conduct regular access reviews.

2. **Gap 2: Inadequate Network Security**  
   - **Description**: Firewalls and intrusion detection systems are not properly configured.  
   - **Remediation Timeline**: 2 months  
   - **Recommendations**: Review and update firewall rules, and enhance intrusion detection capabilities.

3. **Gap 3: Insufficient Encryption Measures**  
   - **Description**: Sensitive data is not encrypted both at rest and in transit.  
   - **Remediation Timeline**: 4 months  
   - **Recommendations**: Implement end-to-end encryption for sensitive data and ensure compliance with cryptographic standards.

## Conclusion
Addressing these compliance gaps is critical to achieving PCI-DSS compliance. Timely remediation and adherence to the recommendations will enhance security posture and reduce the risk of data breaches.
