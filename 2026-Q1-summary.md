# 2026 Q1 Summary

## Overview of Compliance Status

The compliance status for Q1 2026 demonstrates steady progress in meeting regulatory requirements and internal policies. Key compliance areas have been regularly monitored, and efforts to address previous gaps have been implemented effectively.

## Key Findings and Observations

- Improved adherence to data protection policies across departments.
- Successful completion of mandatory security training by 95% of employees.
- Identification of areas requiring enhanced access controls.
- Increased focus on vendor risk management.

## Critical and High Vulnerabilities Summary

During Q1 2026, several critical and high vulnerabilities were identified and addressed promptly:

- Critical vulnerabilities related to outdated software versions on key systems.
- High vulnerabilities found in web application input validation, particularly concerning XSS and SQL Injection risks.
- Vendor systems with potential exposure to unauthorized access.

## Recommendations for Improvement

- Accelerate patch management processes to reduce exposure time.
- Enhance web application security testing and code reviews.
- Strengthen vendor risk assessment procedures and continuous monitoring.
