# Phishing Awareness Campaign Plan

## Objective:
To enhance staff awareness of phishing threats and improve the timely and accurate reporting of phishing incidents through effective incident reporting procedures.

## Campaign Components:

### 1. Awareness Training Sessions
- Conduct interactive training sessions focused on recognizing phishing emails and indicators.
- Include real-world examples and case studies of phishing attempts and their consequences.
- Emphasize the importance of vigilance and immediate action upon suspicion.

### 2. Incident Reporting Protocol Integration
- Introduce the established incident reporting protocols during training.
- Provide step-by-step guidance on how to report suspected phishing emails using the organization's reporting system.
- Clarify roles and responsibilities of staff in the phishing incident reporting process.
- Communicate timelines and expectations for incident reporting and follow-up actions.

### 3. Communication and Reminders
- Distribute regular email reminders highlighting key phishing indicators and reporting procedures.
- Use posters and digital signage in common areas to reinforce awareness messages.
- Share success stories where prompt reporting prevented potential security breaches.

### 4. Simulated Phishing Exercises
- Launch simulated phishing campaigns to test staff awareness and responsiveness.
- Provide immediate feedback and additional training for those who fall for simulations.
- Use results to identify areas for improvement and tailor future training.

### 5. Monitoring and Feedback
- Monitor incident reports related to phishing for trends and response effectiveness.
- Collect feedback from staff on the training and reporting process to continuously improve the campaign.
- Report campaign progress and outcomes to management regularly.

## Timeline:
- Month 1: Develop training materials and incident reporting protocol documentation.
- Month 2: Conduct initial training sessions and launch communication efforts.
- Month 3: Begin simulated phishing exercises and gather feedback.
- Ongoing: Monitor, remind, and refine the campaign based on feedback and incident data.

## Responsibilities:
- Security Awareness Lead: Oversee campaign planning, execution, and monitoring.
- IT Security Team: Support training delivery and incident investigation.
- All Staff: Participate in training, remain vigilant, and report incidents promptly.
