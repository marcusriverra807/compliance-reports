# Vendor Compliance and Security Practices Report

## Compliance Deadlines (from ACTION_PLAN.md)

The organization has established a robust PCI-DSS Compliance Action Plan with clear deadlines to enhance vendor compliance and security practices:

- **May 5, 2026:** Appoint a Compliance Officer to oversee PCI-DSS compliance and address vulnerabilities including SQL Injection and XSS risks.
- **May 10, 2026:** Begin regular reviews of compliance status and update action plans accordingly.
- **June 15, 2026:** Conduct a comprehensive compliance audit focusing on PCI-DSS requirements and remediation of critical vulnerabilities.
- **June 30, 2026:** Implement Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) to regulate access based on job responsibilities.
- **July 2026 (Quarterly):** Initiate regular vulnerability scans and penetration tests targeting critical areas such as SQL Injection and XSS.
- **July 15, 2026 (Quarterly):** Start conducting quarterly access reviews to revoke unnecessary permissions.
- **July 2026:** Launch an ongoing training program for employees on PCI-DSS compliance and specific vulnerability awareness.
- **August 15, 2026:** Update all compliance documentation and establish a monitoring system to track compliance status and regulatory changes.
- **August 15, 2026:** Implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) for sensitive systems.
- **September 1, 2026:** Deploy monitoring tools to detect unauthorized access attempts and generate management reports.
- **December 2026 (Annual):** Conduct annual reviews of access control policies for continuous improvement.
- **Within 6 months:** Complete remediation efforts with regular evaluations to ensure compliance and address emerging threats.

## Recommendations (from 2026-Q1-summary.md)

The Q1 2026 summary highlights key areas for improvement in vendor compliance and security:

- Accelerate patch management processes to minimize exposure to vulnerabilities.
- Enhance web application security testing and code reviews to detect and remediate risks such as XSS and SQL Injection.
- Strengthen vendor risk assessment procedures and implement continuous monitoring to promptly identify and mitigate vendor-related security risks.

## Potential Implications for Vendor Performance Assessment

- **Timely Compliance:** Vendors will be expected to align with the organization's compliance deadlines, particularly regarding access controls, vulnerability remediation, and documentation updates.
- **Security Posture:** Vendors must demonstrate proactive vulnerability management, including regular scanning, patching, and secure authentication mechanisms.
- **Training and Awareness:** Vendors may need to participate in or support employee training programs to ensure understanding of compliance requirements and security best practices.
- **Monitoring and Reporting:** Continuous compliance monitoring and incident reporting will be critical metrics in vendor performance evaluations.
- **Risk Mitigation:** Enhanced vendor risk assessment and monitoring processes will influence vendor selection and ongoing performance reviews, emphasizing security posture and responsiveness to identified risks.
