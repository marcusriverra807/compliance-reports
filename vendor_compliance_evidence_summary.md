# Vendor Compliance and Security Evidence Summary

## Compliance Deadlines
- Appoint a Compliance Officer by May 5, 2026.
- Begin regular compliance reviews by May 10, 2026.
- Conduct a comprehensive PCI-DSS compliance audit by June 15, 2026.
- Implement Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) and conduct quarterly access reviews.
- Initiate regular vulnerability scans and penetration tests quarterly.
- Launch ongoing employee training on PCI-DSS and vulnerability awareness.
- Implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) and deploy monitoring tools for unauthorized access detection.
- Conduct annual access control policy reviews and complete remediation efforts within 6 months.

## Recommendations
- Accelerate patch management to reduce vulnerability exposure.
- Enhance web application security testing and code reviews for vulnerabilities such as XSS and SQL Injection.
- Strengthen vendor risk assessment and continuous monitoring.

## Potential Implications for Vendor Performance Assessment
- Vendors must meet compliance deadlines and demonstrate proactive vulnerability management.
- Participation in training programs and support for compliance awareness is expected.
- Continuous compliance monitoring and incident reporting will be critical metrics.
- Enhanced risk assessment and monitoring will influence vendor selection and reviews.

## PCI-DSS Compliance Updates
- Implemented role-based access control (RBAC) and regular access reviews.
- Utilized strong encryption protocols like AES-256 and TLS 1.2 or higher.
- Conduct regular vulnerability scans, penetration testing, and prompt patching.
- Established monitoring systems and scheduled ongoing staff training on PCI-DSS requirements.

This summary consolidates key vendor compliance and security practice points for future reference and audit purposes.
