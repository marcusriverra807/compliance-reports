# Remediation Process Documentation for High Severity Vulnerabilities

## 1. Remediation Workflow

- Identification: Continuously monitor and identify critical and high severity vulnerabilities through regular scans and assessments.
- Assessment: Conduct detailed analysis and root cause investigation for each identified vulnerability.
- Assignment: Assign dedicated remediation teams based on expertise and system ownership.
- Remediation: Execute remediation activities including patching, configuration updates, and code fixes.
- Verification: Validate remediation through retesting, vulnerability rescans, and code reviews.
- Closure: Officially close vulnerability tickets only after successful remediation validation.

## 2. Reporting and Communication

- Weekly Progress Reviews: Schedule weekly meetings to discuss remediation status and challenges.
- Status Updates: Provide regular status reports to stakeholders via email or compliance dashboards.
- Escalation: Define escalation paths for unresolved or high-risk vulnerabilities.

## 3. Risk Assessment and Prioritization

- Severity Classification: Utilize a standardized severity rating system (e.g., CVSS) to classify vulnerabilities.
- Prioritization Criteria: Prioritize remediation based on severity, exploitability, and business impact.
- Risk Mitigation: Implement interim controls for high risk vulnerabilities pending full remediation.

## 4. Verification and Validation

- Retesting: Conduct retesting after remediation to confirm vulnerability resolution.
- Acceptance Criteria: Define clear criteria for successful remediation closure.
- Documentation: Record validation results and approvals.

## 5. Record Keeping

- Documentation Requirements: Maintain records of remediation activities, evidence, and approvals.
- Retention Period: Retain documentation according to organizational policies and regulatory requirements.
- Audit Readiness: Ensure documentation is organized and accessible for audits.

## 6. Training and Awareness

- Training Programs: Provide regular training sessions on remediation processes and documentation standards.
- Awareness Campaigns: Promote security awareness emphasizing the importance of timely remediation.

---

This documentation update aligns with PCI-DSS requirements and enhances the compliance posture by formalizing the remediation process for high severity vulnerabilities.
