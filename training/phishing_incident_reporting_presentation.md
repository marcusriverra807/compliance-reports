---

- Equip staff with knowledge and skills to identify phishing attempts and effectively report them as part of incident reporting procedures.

## Slide 2: Introduction to Incident Reporting
- Importance of timely and accurate incident reporting for organizational security.
- Overview of incident reporting procedures and designated reporting channels.

**Speaker Notes:**
Incident reporting is critical to our security posture. We'll review how and where to report incidents to ensure quick and effective response.

---

## Slide 3: Understanding Phishing Threats
- Definition of phishing: deceptive attempts to obtain sensitive information via email or other communication.
- Common phishing tactics:
  - Suspicious links and URLs
  - Unexpected attachments
  - Urgent or threatening language
  - Spoofed sender addresses

**Speaker Notes:**
Phishing is a major threat. Recognizing common tactics helps us stay vigilant and protect our data.

---

## Slide 4: Recognizing Phishing Emails
- Visual cues: misspellings, poor grammar, inconsistent branding.
- Behavioral cues: unexpected requests for credentials, financial information, or confidential data.
- Technical cues: hovering over links to verify URLs, checking sender email addresses carefully.

**Speaker Notes:**
Learn to spot the signs of phishing emails through visual, behavioral, and technical clues.

---

## Slide 5: Reporting Phishing Incidents
- Step-by-step guide to reporting suspected phishing:
  - Do not click any links or open attachments.
  - Forward the suspicious email to the designated security team or use the internal reporting tool.
  - Provide any relevant details or context.
- Importance of reporting even if unsure.

**Speaker Notes:**
Reporting is vital. Even if unsure, report suspicious emails immediately to help us respond quickly.
